My name is Rees Draminski, and I am an undergraduate currently studying Philosophy and Computer Science at the University of Maryland, Baltimore County (UMBC). I plan to graduate in May 2022.

My interests, in no particular order, include, but are not limited to:

  • Philosophy of Technology
  • Computer Science Education (CSEd)
  • Cross-Platform Webapp Development
  • Gamification
  • Linguistics
  • Evolution / Evolutionary Psychology

I most recently served as the Director of Technology for TeCanal, a 501(c)(3) nonprofit dedicated to providing STEM education and technology resources to underserved communities. I was previously the CTO (2017) and CEO (2018) of this organization, before becoming the Director of Technology until 2021, when the organization dissolved. In these roles, I spent my time developing curriculum and technological tools to teach Computer Science in underresourced schools, most of the time with limited Internet connections. As a result of my work with TeCanal, I was awarded the Baltimore Ravens Community Quarterback Award in 2017 and the Daily Point of Light Award in 2018.

In the past, I was a part of the Inaugural Microsoft Council for Digital Good which was comprised of 15 students from around the U.S. We met around and discussed digital civility, social media, privacy, and other Internet-related topics. With this organization, I was able to speak at a UNICEF-Microsoft joint hosted panel called Empowering Children in a Digital Age. When Microsoft published their 5th annual Digital Civility Index Report for 2020, I was interviewed about my experience with the Council, as well my thoughts about why Digital Civility increased so much as a result of youth activity.
